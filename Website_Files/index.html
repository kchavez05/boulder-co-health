Boulder County Health Inspection Scores

vs. Google Business Ratings

If you are not familiar with the inner workings of the restaurant industry, you probably know that mandatory–and usually, surprise–health inspections performed by a local representative are a necessary part of the opening, owning, and maintaining foodservice establishment. Federal, state, and local food safety standards ensure that your dine-in and take-out experiences don’t end in disaster.

But what if they did anyway? Did you head straight to Google and write a scathing review?

And what about those exemplary experiences–those true dining stand-outs? Did you give the same treatment to those restaurants as you did to the one that forced you to stay 10 feet from your nearest bathroom?

Just as health inspections gauge and regulate the quality of a restaurant by numbers from a local government perspective, the reviews submitted by the average consumer might be a better judge of a restaurant’s character.

Our team decided to build a model to test the relationship between the health inspection scores and Google ratings of restaurants across Boulder County. We wished to know more about the correlation between these two distantly-related numbers. Please explore our data and our subsequent findings.
